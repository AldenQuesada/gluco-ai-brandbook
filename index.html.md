# 2. LOGO SYSTEM

## 2.1 Concept A -- "The Waveform" (Minimal)

### Description
A continuous, fluid waveform line that abstracts a glucose curve into a single elegant stroke. The line rises from left in a smooth sinusoidal ascent, peaks at a precise apex, and descends into a controlled taper. The peak is subtly sharpened -- not a raw spike, but a refined point that signals precision. The line's weight varies: thinner at the extremes (calm baseline), thicker at the peak (metabolic activity). The wordmark "GLUCO" sits to the right in a geometric sans-serif, tracked wide. "AI" appears as a superscript in a contrasting weight or the accent color.

### Construction
- **Grid:** 24x24 unit grid, golden ratio proportions (1:1.618) governing the wave's amplitude-to-width
- **Stroke:** Variable weight from 2px to 4px (at 48px height), rounded terminals
- **Apex angle:** 142 degrees (softer than medical, sharper than decorative)
- **Curve:** Cubic bezier with control points at 0.33 and 0.66 of horizontal span
- **Baseline:** Extends 15% beyond wave endpoints as a grounding element

### Symbolism
The waveform represents continuous glucose monitoring rendered as art. The variable stroke weight communicates that GLUCO AI reads nuance, not just data points. The controlled peak signals that metabolic spikes are understood and managed, not feared.

## 2.2 Concept B -- "The Hexagon Pulse" (Tech)

### Description
A regular hexagon (rotated 30 degrees so a flat edge sits at top and bottom) with an internal glucose pulse line cutting through it horizontally. The hexagon references molecular structure (glucose is C6H12O6 -- a six-carbon molecule). The pulse line enters from the left edge of the hexagon, traverses with a single clean oscillation (one valley, one peak), and exits through the right edge. The hexagon's edges use a subtle gradient stroke that shifts from Metabolic Blue at top-left to Biometric Teal at bottom-right. The wordmark "GLUCO" appears below or beside the hexagon in a technical sans-serif. "AI" is rendered in the accent teal within a small pill-shaped badge.

### Construction
- **Grid:** Hexagon inscribed in a 48x48 unit circle
- **Hexagon:** 2px uniform stroke, 30-degree rotation, interior angles at 120 degrees
- **Pulse line:** 2.5px stroke, enters at left midpoint, valley at 35% horizontal, peak at 65% horizontal, exits at right midpoint
- **Valley depth:** 30% of hexagon interior height
- **Peak height:** 45% of hexagon interior height (asymmetric to signal "growth" not just "reading")
- **Gradient:** Linear, 135 degrees, Metabolic Blue (#0066FF) to Biometric Teal (#00D4AA)

### Symbolism
The hexagon is glucose's molecular identity rendered as a tech-forward container. The internal pulse proves this is not abstract chemistry but living, breathing biometric data. The asymmetric peak-to-valley ratio signals optimization -- the system trends upward.

### Variations

| **Horizontal** | Icon left, wordmark right, aligned to hexagon center |

## 2.3 Concept C -- "The Glyph" (Health-Tech Fusion)

### Description
A custom letterform "G" that integrates a glucose data visualization within its counter (the open interior space of the letter). The "G" is constructed as a three-quarter circle (opening at the right side, like a standard G) with a geometric, slightly squared treatment inspired by DIN and Eurostile. Inside the counter, a miniature area chart rises from the bottom of the G's interior -- a filled gradient shape representing metabolic data over time, with a single clean peak. The crossbar of the G extends inward and becomes the "baseline" of this internal chart. This creates a letter that IS the product -- the G literally contains metabolic intelligence.

### Construction
- **Grid:** 48x48 unit grid
- **G outer stroke:** 5px weight, three-quarter arc (270 degrees), opening faces 3 o'clock
- **G crossbar:** Extends inward from the opening, 40% of the G's width
- **Interior chart:** Filled area from crossbar baseline upward, peak at 60% of counter height
- **Chart fill:** Gradient from Metabolic Blue (bottom, 80% opacity) to Biometric Teal (top, 40% opacity)
- **Corner treatment:** 2px radius on crossbar terminal, smooth arc connections elsewhere

### Symbolism
The G is both letter and living dashboard. It communicates that GLUCO AI does not just monitor -- it embeds intelligence into the very identity of the brand. The internal area chart filling the counter suggests data that is always present, always working, always rising.

### Variations

| Variant | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Primary** | G-glyph icon + "LUCO AI" wordmark (the G IS the icon), horizontal |
| **Horizontal** | G-glyph as first letter, "LUCO AI" continues in matching typeface |
| **Icon only** | G-glyph standalone, minimum 24px (internal chart simplifies to solid fill at <32px) |
| **Dark mode** | G stroke in Glacier White, internal chart gradient preserved, "LUCO AI" in white |
| **Light mode** | G stroke in Deep Obsidian, internal chart gradient preserved, "LUCO AI" in Charcoal |
| **Favicon** | G-glyph with solid internal fill (no gradient detail) at 16x16 |
| **App icon** | G-glyph centered on dark gradient background, chart gradient vivid, 1024x1024 |

## 2.4 Recommended Logo

**Primary recommendation: Concept C -- "The Glyph"**

Rationale: It achieves the highest brand recall by fusing the brand name with the product experience. It is distinctive at every size, works as both icon and integrated wordmark, and has no close competitors in the health-tech space. The internal data visualization makes the logo a perpetual product demo.

**Secondary/alternate: Concept A -- "The Waveform"** for contexts requiring maximum simplicity (partnerships, co-branding, regulatory filings).

# 4. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

## 4.1 Primary Typeface -- Headings

### **Inter**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Family** | Inter |
| **Designer** | Rasmus Andersson |
| **Classification** | Geometric Neo-Grotesque Sans-Serif |
| **Weights used** | Medium (500), SemiBold (600), Bold (700) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts / Variable font |
| **License** | SIL Open Font License |

**Rationale:** Inter was designed specifically for computer screens with features optimized for UI: tabular figures for data alignment, contextual alternates for readability at small sizes, and an open aperture that maintains legibility in the 11-14px range where biometric data lives. Its geometric construction signals tech precision while its humanist curves prevent clinical coldness. Used by Linear, Vercel, and Raycast -- signaling "premium developer/power-user tool." Compared to alternatives: SF Pro is Apple-locked; Helvetica Neue feels dated for health-tech; Satoshi lacks tabular figures.

---

## 4.2 Secondary Typeface -- Body

### **Inter**

Same family, lower weights.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Weights used** | Regular (400), Medium (500) |
| **Line height** | 1.5 for body text, 1.6 for long-form |

**Rationale:** Using a single type family with weight differentiation reduces cognitive load and download cost. Inter's weight axis provides sufficient contrast between heading (600-700) and body (400-500) without needing a second family. This is the Apple Health / Linear approach: one family, many weights, maximum coherence.

---

## 4.3 Data/Mono Typeface

### **JetBrains Mono**

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Family** | JetBrains Mono |
| **Designer** | Philipp Nurullin, Konstantin Bulenkov |
| **Classification** | Monospace |
| **Weights used** | Regular (400), Medium (500), Bold (700) |
| **Source** | Google Fonts / JetBrains |
| **License** | SIL Open Font License |

**Rationale:** For glucose readings, metabolic scores, and data-dense displays, monospace alignment is critical. JetBrains Mono's increased x-height and distinctive zero (dotted or slashed) prevent digit confusion in medical-adjacent displays (critical: 0 vs O). Its slightly playful character shapes prevent the "terminal screen" coldness that IBM Plex Mono or SF Mono can project. This typeface says "data is exciting, not intimidating."

## 4.6 Typography Rules

1. **Never use more than 3 weights** on a single screen/page
2. **Data values always use JetBrains Mono** -- never Inter for numbers in data contexts
3. **Negative tracking only at display/h1 sizes** (32px+) -- body text uses 0 or positive
4. **Minimum readable size:** 12px / 0.75rem (11px only for uppercase overlines)
5. **Maximum line length:** 72 characters for body text (optimal: 60-66)
6. **Paragraph spacing:** 1.5x the body line height (24px for 16px body)

## 5.4 Motion Principles

### Core Motion Values

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Purposeful** | Every animation communicates state change. No decoration-only motion. |
| **Metabolic** | Motion rhythm mirrors biological cadence: smooth entry, organic easing, no hard stops. |
| **Responsive** | Interactions respond within 100ms. Transitions complete within 300ms. |
| **Reduced** | Honor `prefers-reduced-motion`. All animations are optional enhancements. |

### Duration Tokens

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `duration-instant` | 100ms | Micro-interactions: hover, focus, color change |
| `duration-fast` | 200ms | Button press, toggle, small reveals |

| `duration-slow` | 500ms | Complex reveals, chart data loading |

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|

## 5.5 Photography Direction

### Style

| Property | Guideline |
|----------|-----------|
| **Overall tone** | Editorial premium. Think: Kinfolk meets Wired. Natural light bias. |
| **Color treatment** | Desaturated 15-20% from reality. Cool-neutral white balance (5800K-6200K). |
| **Composition** | Generous negative space (40%+ of frame). Subject positioned at golden ratio intersection points. |
| **Focus** | Shallow depth of field (f/1.8-f/2.8) for people. Deep DOF (f/8-f/11) for product. |
| **Lighting** | Natural window light preferred. If artificial: large, soft sources mimicking north-facing window. |

### Subject Guidelines

| Subject | Direction | Avoid |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **People** | Diverse, fit-not-bodybuilder, 35-55, in context (kitchen, office, gym). Candid over posed. Eye contact with camera OR deeply focused on task. | Stock poses, forced smiles, lab coats, hospital settings |
| **Devices/Product** | Shot on dark surfaces (matte black, dark wood). Minimal props. Angled 15-30 degrees. Screen showing actual UI. | Floating device renders, too many props, colored backgrounds |
| **Food** | Overhead or 30-degree angle. Whole foods, meal prep aesthetic. Moody lighting. | Fast food, overly styled food, bright/saturated food photography |
| **Lifestyle** | Morning routines, focused work, athletic performance, meditation. Real environments. | Party scenes, alcohol, extreme sports, anything suggesting illness |

### Photography Don'ts

1. Never show needles, blood, or invasive medical procedures
2. Never use blue-tinted "tech" filters (the brand blue lives in UI, not photography)
3. Never show the CGM sensor in a way that emphasizes the medical/needle aspect
4. Never use images where the subject appears unhealthy or distressed
5. Never use images with pure white backgrounds (use off-white/neutral-50 minimum)

|----------|--------------|

| **Y axis** | JetBrains Mono `type-data-xs`, labels at 50 mg/dL intervals |
| **Data points** | No visible points by default. On hover: 6px circle with 2px white border, filled with zone color |
| **Annotations** | Small pills above chart line for meals/activity/insulin, using category icons at 16px |

| Property | Direction |

| **Type** | Geometric/abstract only. No figurative illustration. |

# 7. BRAND GUIDELINES SUMMARY

## 7.1 Logo Usage

### Do's

| Rule | Specification |
|------|--------------|
| Maintain minimum clear space | Equal to the height of the "G" in the wordmark on all sides |
| Use approved color variants only | Primary, dark mode, light mode, monochrome white, monochrome black |
| Maintain aspect ratio | Never stretch, skew, or compress |
| Minimum size -- horizontal logo | 120px width (digital), 30mm (print) |
| Minimum size -- icon only | 16px (digital), 5mm (print) |
| Place on solid backgrounds | Single-color backgrounds or approved gradients |
| Use the icon variant for small sizes | Below 120px width, switch to icon-only |

### Don'ts

| Violation | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| Never place logo on busy photography without a container | Legibility loss |
| Never apply shadows, gradients, or effects to the logo itself | Brand integrity |
| Never rotate the logo | Breaks the "G-contains-data" narrative |
| Never use unapproved colors | Dilutes brand recognition |
| Never recreate or approximate the logo | Always use supplied vector files |
| Never place on a background with < 4.5:1 contrast ratio | Accessibility (WCAG AA) |
| Never animate the logo | The logo is a static mark (the product is dynamic, the brand is stable) |
| Never lock up with other logos without minimum 2x clear space | Co-branding clarity |

## 7.2 Color Combination Rules

### Approved Combinations

| Background | Primary Text | Secondary Text | Accent | WCAG Ratio |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|--------|------------|
| Deep Obsidian (`#0A1628`) | Glacier White (`#F0F4F8`) | `neutral-400` (`#9AA5B4`) | Metabolic Blue / Biometric Teal | 15.2:1 / 8.4:1 |
| `dark-surface-2` (`#131F33`) | Glacier White | `neutral-400` | Metabolic Blue | 14.1:1 / 7.8:1 |
| White (`#FFFFFF`) | `neutral-800` (`#1A2332`) | `neutral-600` (`#4A5568`) | Metabolic Blue | 14.8:1 / 7.2:1 |
| `neutral-50` (`#F7F9FC`) | `neutral-800` | `neutral-600` | Metabolic Blue | 14.3:1 / 6.9:1 |
| Metabolic Blue (`#0066FF`) | White | `primary-100` (`#CCE0FF`) | Biometric Teal | 4.6:1 / 3.1:1* |

*`primary-100` on Metabolic Blue is for decorative/non-essential text only -- it passes AA Large but not AA Normal.

### Forbidden Combinations

| Combination | Reason |
|------------|--------|
| Metabolic Blue text on Deep Obsidian | Contrast 3.2:1 -- fails WCAG AA. Use `primary-400` (`#3385FF`) instead: 5.1:1 |
| Biometric Teal text on white backgrounds | Contrast 2.8:1 -- fails all levels. Use `teal-700` (`#008066`) instead: 4.6:1 |
| Alert Red on Metabolic Blue | Visual vibration, inaccessible |
| Any state color as a background with white text | Most fail contrast; state colors are for indicators, not surfaces |
| `neutral-400` text on `neutral-50` background | Contrast 3.8:1 -- fails AA Normal. Use `neutral-500` or darker |

### Gradient Rules

| Gradient | Start | End | Direction | Usage |
|----------|-------|-----|-----------|-------|
| **Primary gradient** | `#0066FF` | `#00D4AA` | 135 degrees | Logo fills, hero backgrounds, premium accents |
| **Dark gradient** | `#0A1628` | `#131F33` | 180 degrees (top to bottom) | Page backgrounds, depth layers |
| **Surface gradient** | `#131F33` | `#1A2B45` | 180 degrees | Card hover states, elevated surfaces |
| **Alert gradient** | `#FF3B5C` | `#FFB020` | 90 degrees | Critical alert backgrounds (sparingly) |

**Gradient rules:**
- Maximum 2 colors per gradient (never 3+)
- Never use gradients on text (except display-size headlines in marketing materials)
- Gradients on buttons: only the primary CTA, only the primary gradient
- Never apply gradients to the logo mark itself

## 7.3 Typography Hierarchy Rules

### Heading Hierarchy (per page/screen)

| Rule | Specification |
|------|--------------|
| One `type-display` per page maximum | Hero/landing pages only |
| One `type-h1` per page | Page title |
| Multiple `type-h2` allowed | Section headers, maintain logical order |
| `type-h3`/`type-h4` nest under their parent | Never use H3 without a preceding H2 |
| Data types are parallel, not hierarchical | `type-data-xl` can coexist with `type-h2` |

### Weight Restrictions

| Context | Max Weights | Allowed |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Mobile screen | 3 | Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600) |
| Desktop page | 3 | Regular (400), SemiBold (600), Bold (700) |
| Data dashboard | 3 | Regular (400), Medium (500), Bold (700) |

### Data Display Rules

1. All numeric data in context (glucose readings, scores, percentages) must use JetBrains Mono
2. Units of measurement ("mg/dL", "%", "bpm") use `type-caption` in `neutral-400`, never the same size as the number
3. Tabular data must use tabular figures (Inter and JetBrains Mono both support `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`)
4. Decimal points in glucose data: always 0 decimal places for mg/dL, 1 decimal place for mmol/L
5. Large numbers (>999) use commas as thousand separators in en-US locale

## 7.5 Accessibility Standards

### WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance (Mandatory)

| Criterion | Requirement | GLUCO AI Implementation |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------|
| **1.4.3** Text Contrast | 4.5:1 normal, 3:1 large text | All text/background pairs tested and documented in 7.2 |
| **1.4.11** Non-text Contrast | 3:1 for UI components | All interactive elements, chart lines, and icons exceed 3:1 |
| **1.4.1** Color Not Sole Indicator | Information not conveyed by color alone | Glucose zones use color + text label + icon. Chart includes pattern fills option. |
| **2.1.1** Keyboard Accessible | All functionality via keyboard | Tab order defined for all interactive components |
| **2.4.7** Focus Visible | Visible focus indicators | 2px Metabolic Blue outline, 2px offset, on all focusable elements |
| **1.3.1** Info and Relationships | Semantic HTML structure | Heading hierarchy matches visual hierarchy |
| **1.4.4** Resize Text | Up to 200% without loss | Responsive type scale, rem-based sizing |

### Focus State Design

| Element | Focus Style |
|---------|------------|
| Buttons | 2px solid `#0066FF`, 2px offset, `radius-md` on outline |
| Links | 2px solid `#0066FF`, 2px offset, text underline persists |
| Inputs | 2px solid `#0066FF`, replaces default border |
| Cards (interactive) | 2px solid `#0066FF`, 2px offset, `radius-lg` on outline |
| Chart data points | 3px solid ring + tooltip auto-opens |

### Color Blind Considerations

| Type | Population | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|------------|
| Deuteranopia (red-green) | ~6% males | Glucose zones use distinct luminance values: Red at 62% lightness, Amber at 56%, Green at 39%. Shape indicators supplement color. |
| Protanopia (red-weak) | ~1% males | Alert Red (`#FF3B5C`) and Optimal Green (`#00C853`) differ by 23% lightness AND hue direction |
| Tritanopia (blue-yellow) | ~0.01% | Metabolic Blue and Signal Amber differ by 177 hue degrees -- distinct even with blue-yellow confusion |

### Alternative Modes

| Mode | Treatment |
|------|-----------|
| **High contrast** | Respect `prefers-contrast: more`. Increase all borders to 2px. Remove glassmorphism. Increase shadow opacity. |
| **Reduced motion** | Respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Replace animations with instant state changes. Glucose chart line appears fully drawn. |
| **Dark mode** | System default for app. Respect `prefers-color-scheme`. Full token mapping in Section 3.5. |
